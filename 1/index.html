CS180 Project 1: Colorizing the Prokudin-Gorskii photo collection

Introduction

Sergey Prokudin-Gorsky was russian photographer, who was known to for his effort to document 20th century Russia. He traveled Russia and took photos of a wide range of stuff and had the idea to record every scene onto a glass plate with a red, blue and green filter each. This way he could use a projector with different lighting, and the 3 glassplates in different colors, to project a color image.

Single-scale implementation with successful results on low-res images

Process: - Load image and slice into 3 seperate equal height images, one for blue green and red respectively (blue as base) - Use NCC, on the intensities which was better than euclidean loss, to evaluate whether the images match - use np.roll to try out different displacements - before we calculate the NCC, we apply the NCC only on a cropped middle section, since corners are problematic - play around with cropping parameters and displacement pixel ranges - select the displacement with the best NCC - Result:
